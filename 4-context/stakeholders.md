# Stakeholders

Use this file to maintain a small, evidence-backed map of internal product stakeholders and groups that influence product decisions, need updates, or repeatedly ask for product information.

Stakeholder memory should help Mole answer questions such as:

- Who cares about this product area?
- What has this person or group asked for before?
- What decisions can they make or influence?
- How should product updates be tailored for them?
- Which org-chart relationships matter for product work?

## Inbox synthesis rules

When synthesising `6-raw/inbox/`:

1. Check whether each substantive internal signal mentions a stakeholder, function, leadership group, decision forum, reporting relationship, product concern, update preference, or recurring ask.
2. Update a matching stakeholder profile when the signal clarifies role, authority, influence, product interests, decision criteria, preferred cadence, preferred format, communication channel, concerns, open asks, or org-chart relationships.
3. Create a new stakeholder section only when the input points to a meaningfully distinct person, group, committee, or function that is not represented here.
4. Add source links to the originating inbox item, evidence note, source document, meeting note, decision, or signal cluster.
5. Preserve uncertainty. Mark weak, inferred, or early signals as hypotheses instead of facts.
6. Update relevant summaries or indexes only when the stakeholder signal materially changes reusable product context.
